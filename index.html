About Me

I am a passionate researcher with a strong foundation in Computer Science and Data Science, currently working at the Microbiome Informatics Lab in IIIT Delhi. My academic path began with a B.Tech in Computer Science and Engineering from Techno International, Newtown (2023)(undertaken by Maulana Abul Kalam Azad University of Technology), followed by research roles at IISER Bhopal and IIT Guwahati. These experiences deepened my interest in computational approaches to health and biomedical data, especially in Machine Learning and Deep Learning for Healthcare.

My research interests span Metagenomics, Bioinformatics, and Biological Data Analysis, with a growing focus on Scalable Data Science and the intersection of Computer Architecture with Computational Biology. I enjoy working in collaborative, interdisciplinary environments and am driven by the goal of developing efficient computational solutions to complex biological challenges.

Beyond research, I mentor GATE aspirants in Computer Science and Data Science (https://topmate.io/debarshi_roy) and serve as a motivational speaker. My hobbies include playing cricket and chess, swimming, drama, and reading non-fiction and spiritual literature.

Research

My research focuses on the intersection of Computer Science and Biology, particularly in applying machine learning and statistical models to analyze biological datasets.

Research Interests

  • Computational Metagenomics and Human Microbiome Analysis
  • Bioinformatics and Disease Prediction using Machine Learning
  • Biomedical Natural Language Processing (BioNLP)
  • Scalable Data Science & Computer Architecture in Bioinformatics
  • Lightweight Cryptographic Protocols for Healthcare IoT Systems

Recent Projects

Disease-Oriented Analysis of Human Microbiome Metadata (IIIT Delhi)

  • Used R and Python to clean and analyze ~55,978 samples of microbiome metadata
  • Applied statistical tests and visualizations to uncover disease-specific patterns
  • Developing an LLM-based framework for predictive modeling in biomedical domains

Scalable and Secure Consensus (IIT Guwahati)

  • Explored quorum reduction and hybrid encryption techniques in Oblivious Paxos
  • Applied grid quorum theory and cryptographic primitives to improve scalability

Surveys on Prefetching & Lightweight Cryptography

  • Authored detailed surveys analyzing secure and adaptive caching mechanisms
  • Evaluated lightweight encryption methods for constrained IoT environments

Future Aspirations

My long-term goal is to pursue a Ph.D. in Computational Biology, with a focused research trajectory in Computational Metagenomics. I am particularly interested in developing scalable, data-driven pipelines for analyzing human microbiome datasets, integrating clinical metadata, and applying machine learning and statistical methods to extract meaningful biological insights. I aim to contribute to advancements in disease classification, biomarker discovery, and precision medicine through robust computational approaches that bridge biology and data science.
